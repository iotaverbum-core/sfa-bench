# Objection handling

Honest and sharp. Never claim a certification or guarantee that isn't built.

| Objection | Response |
|---|---|
| **"We already have monitoring."** | Monitoring helps you improve the app with editable dashboards. This produces a reproducible, tamper-evident record an outsider can verify. Different job: iteration vs. evidence. You probably still can't hand your current tool's output to a prospect's auditor. |
| **"We're not using agents yet."** | This isn't for autonomous agents - it's for document-grounded *answers*. If your assistant cites sources, it applies today. |
| **"We do this manually."** | Manual review isn't reproducible, doesn't scale to every answer, and can't prove the record wasn't edited later. We make it deterministic and sealed; you keep humans on the findings we surface. |
| **"We don't have budget."** | It's $1,500-2,500, scoped to unblock a deal worth far more stuck in review. If it finds nothing, you get a clean, reproducible baseline you can show buyers. |
| **"This feels like consulting."** | Fixed scope, fixed price, fixed timeline. The deliverable is a reproducible artifact plus a tool you keep running - not a slide deck. We do one rule-pack tuning pass, no open-ended custom work. |
| **"How is this different from LLM evals?"** | Evals run a model to judge a model: nondeterministic, can hallucinate, mutable dashboard. Ours is a deterministic rule check - same input, same verdict - sealed into a record your auditor re-runs and gets the identical result. It also runs in your VPC; most eval SaaS ingests your prompts and documents. |
| **"What if the pilot finds nothing?"** | Then you get a reproducible, tamper-evident groundedness baseline you can hand a prospect - a sellable result. We state coverage honestly (free-text checks are conservative), so "nothing found" is scoped, not a false all-clear. |
| **"Can we wait until the platform is finished?"** | The value - the audit artifact - exists today, and your deal review is now. The pilot uses the product as it stands; the roadmap doesn't change what you get. |
| **"Is our data safe?"** | It runs in your environment with no network egress and no third-party dependencies - we don't receive your documents or answers. "Tamper-evident" means a covered edit breaks an integrity check we report; it is not a tamper-proof or non-repudiation guarantee, and we don't claim a security certification. |
| **"Who inside our company should own this?"** | Usually whoever owns the stalled deal or AI-risk response - Head of AI or CTO at your stage, sometimes an AI-governance/trust owner. The report is built to satisfy whoever security review routes it to. |

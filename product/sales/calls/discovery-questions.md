# 20 discovery questions

Grouped by theme. The **5 most important** are marked with a star and italics -
if you only get five questions, ask those.

## Current AI / automation use

1. *What does your assistant answer, and from which documents?* ⭐
2. Is it customer-facing or internal?
3. Roughly how many answers per day/week?
4. Do answers cite sources, or is it free text? ⭐

## Current monitoring / evaluation

5. What's in your eval/QA setup today - LLM-as-judge, manual, something else?
6. How often do you run it, and who looks at the results?
7. Could you reproduce a specific answer's groundedness verdict from three weeks ago? ⭐
8. Do you trust the score enough to show it to a customer?

## Failure history

9. When the assistant gets something wrong, how do you find out?
10. Has a wrong or fabricated answer ever reached a customer? What happened?
11. What's the most expensive mistake the assistant could make in your domain?
12. Have you ever had to explain an AI answer after the fact?

## Audit / compliance

13. *Walk me through the last security or AI-risk review - what did they ask about hallucinations or audit trails?* ⭐
14. Has a prospect asked for an audit trail of AI decisions? What did you send?
15. Are you pursuing SOC 2 / ISO 42001 or answering EU AI Act questions?
16. Can customer documents leave your environment, or must they stay in your VPC?

## Team ownership

17. Who owns the answer to "is the assistant safe to ship"?
18. Who responds to security questionnaires today?
19. Is there an AI governance / trust owner, or is it on engineering?

## Budget and urgency

20. *If this unblocked a stalled deal, who owns the budget, and what would you spend to de-risk it?* ⭐

---

**Listening for a strong fit:** an unprompted mention of a stalled/lost deal, a
named budget owner, structured-or-cited answers, and "we can't reproduce that."

The problem

Your assistant answers customer and policy questions from documents. Sometimes it cites a clause that isn't there, or states a number the source contradicts. You find out when a customer complains - or when a prospect's security team asks how you handle it.

Silent failures

Fabricated citations and contradicted facts don't throw errors. They pass review and reach the customer.

Unverifiable outputs

Your evals run a model to grade a model - nondeterministic, editable, and no auditor accepts the score.

No audit trail

When a deal review or regulator asks for a record, you can't reconstruct one after the fact.

Why now

Enterprise procurement now ships AI-risk questionnaires, and EU AI Act record-keeping and ISO 42001 efforts are landing on vendors this year. The question has shifted from "is it good?" to "prove it - and prove you didn't fudge the proof." Every week without a defensible record is another week of answers you can't stand behind.

What the pilot checks

  • Fabricated citations - answers citing source ids that aren't in the evidence.
  • Contradictions - a stated rate, fee, deductible, or date that disagrees with the source.
  • Unverifiable answers - missing citations or claims that make grounding impossible to check.
  • A reproducible groundedness rate across your real answers.
  • A tamper-evident record of every verdict, independently re-runnable.

Honest scope: for free-text answers the check is deterministic and conservative - it reliably flags fabricated citations and contradictions on facts your evidence covers, and under-reports novel claims rather than guessing. Structured, cited answers get the strongest coverage. "Tamper-evident" means a covered edit breaks an integrity check we report; it is not a tamper-proof or certified-compliance guarantee.

FAQ

How is this different from LLM evals?
Evals run a model to judge a model: nondeterministic, can hallucinate, and live in a mutable dashboard. Ours is a deterministic rule check - same input, same verdict, every time - sealed into a record your auditor re-runs and gets the identical result.
What if we already have monitoring?
Monitoring helps you improve the app. This produces a reproducible, tamper-evident record an outsider can verify. Different job: iteration vs. evidence.
What if the pilot finds nothing?
Then you get a reproducible groundedness baseline you can hand a prospect - a sellable result. We state coverage honestly, so "nothing found" is scoped, not a false all-clear.
Do we need to share sensitive data?
No. It runs in your environment with no network egress and no third-party dependencies. We don't receive your documents or answers.
Is this consulting or software?
Software, delivered with a fixed scope. The deliverable is a reproducible artifact plus a tool you keep running - not a slide deck. We do one rule-pack tuning pass, no open-ended custom work.
Who should be involved?
Whoever owns the stalled deal or AI-risk response - usually your Head of AI or CTO - plus one technical contact for the data.
What happens after the pilot?
If it finds risk worth watching, you convert to a monthly subscription and the pilot fee is credited. If not, you keep the report.
How quickly can we start?
As soon as you can share ~200 answers and their evidence - usually within a day of the discovery call.

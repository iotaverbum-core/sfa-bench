GroundLedger Audit Pilot

Prove your AI didn't make it up - in two weeks, on your own answers.

The problem

Your assistant answers customer and policy questions from documents. Sometimes it cites a clause that isn't there, or states a number the source contradicts. You find out when a customer complains - or when a prospect's security team asks how you prevent and evidence it. Evals can't help: they run a model to grade a model, the score moves run to run, and no auditor accepts it.

The pilot

A two-week, fixed-scope engagement that runs deterministic groundedness checks on your assistant's real answers and produces a tamper-evident report your buyer's security team can reproduce. Runs in your environment - no documents leave your walls, no model calls, no network egress.

What we analyse

  • Fabricated citations - cites source ids not in the evidence.
  • Contradictions - a rate/fee/date that disagrees with the source.
  • Unverifiable answers - missing citations/claims.
  • A reproducible groundedness rate, sealed in a tamper-evident ledger.

What you receive

  • One-page executive summary (rate + headline risk).
  • Severity-ranked findings with recommended actions.
  • Evidence per finding (sealed receipt + source).
  • A signed bundle your auditor re-runs offline.
  • Monitoring recommendation + subscription proposal.

Head of AI / founding engineer at a 20-150 person insurance or fintech company shipping a document-grounded assistant into regulated buyers, with a deal slowed by AI-risk review.
